# Design Context

## Target audience

- Solo Pro first: independent researchers, operators, analysts, and small advisory teams who need to explain market, policy, supply-chain, and incident events repeatedly.
- Primary working context: desktop or laptop, long-form review sessions, often during live developments where source reliability and change-tracking matter.

## Use cases

- Start from a live or queued run and inspect the evolving causal graph instead of reading a single blob of AI text.
- Review evidence, degraded-source states, and challenge coverage before exporting or sharing a brief.
- Compare what changed between runs and decide whether a new explanation is ready to forward.

## Brand personality and tone

- High-trust
- Research-grade
- Calm under pressure
- Precise, not flashy
- Operator-facing rather than consumer-social

## Product direction for the Pro kickoff

- Keep only one hard requirement from the OSS product: the knowledge graph must stay the primary surface.
- Do not inherit the OSS evidence-board layout, left/right rails, or sticky-note composition just because they already exist.
- Reimagine the Pro shell as a graph-first command room: spatial, cinematic, and reviewable, with supporting detail revealed through overlays, docks, and focused surfaces.
- Desktop-first for the first shell; mobile support matters later, but the first impression should optimize for serious review work.
- Prefer dark editorial surfaces with warm neutrals, brass accents, and restrained signal colors over neon, gradients, glassmorphism, or generic dashboard tropes.
